# 📊 CPI Inflation Analysis in India (Excel Project)

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes the **Consumer Price Index (CPI) Inflation trends in India** using Excel-based data analytics and visualization techniques. The dataset, sourced from Government of India CPI records, covers inflation trends across categories such as **Food, Health, Housing, Transport, Fuel, Household Services, and more**.

The objective of this project was to identify:

* Key inflation drivers in India
* Pre- and post-COVID inflation patterns
* Food inflation behavior
* Impact of global fuel prices on CPI categories
* Category-wise contribution toward overall inflation

The project was completed entirely using **Microsoft Excel**, including Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, conditional formatting, feature engineering, and correlation analysis.

# 🎯 Why This Dataset & Problem Statements?

I chose this dataset because inflation directly impacts economic stability and household expenditure. CPI data provides a real-world opportunity to analyze:

* Consumer spending behavior
* Inflationary trends
* Economic disruptions like COVID-19
* Fuel-price-driven inflation

The problem statements were selected to perform both:

* **Macro-level analysis** → overall CPI trends
* **Micro-level analysis** → category-specific inflation drivers

This helped create a complete inflation analysis case study using practical business questions.

---

# ⚠️ Challenges Faced During Analysis

### 1. Understanding the General Index

One major challenge was interpreting the **General Index**, since CPI values are indices and not direct price measures. Understanding how inflation is represented relative to a base year required additional research.

### 2. Broad Classification of Categories

The dataset contained multiple detailed categories which needed to be grouped into broader classifications such as:

* Food
* Housing
* Health
* Transport
* Fuel & Light
* Household Services

Creating meaningful category mappings was necessary for higher-level analysis.

### 3. Handling CPI Data Correctly

Since CPI values:

* cannot be summed,
* are index-based,
* and represent average price changes,

it was important to use appropriate aggregations like:

* averages
* and percentage changes,
  instead of totals.

### 4. Missing & Inconsistent Values

Some records contained missing values and inconsistent formatting, which required data cleaning and standardization before analysis. The dataset was cleaned and transformed using the Power Query Editor to ensure consistency and accuracy for further analysis.

# 📈 Key Insights Drawn From the Analysis

## 🥗 Food Was the Largest CPI Contributor

Food accounted for the highest contribution toward CPI, indicating that inflation in India is heavily influenced by essential consumption items.

---

## 🥬 Vegetables & Meat Were Major Food Inflation Drivers

Within food categories:

* Meat & Fish
* Vegetables

showed the highest CPI values, indicating stronger inflationary pressure compared to staples.

---

## 📉 Inflation Peaked in 2022

The highest inflation levels were observed in 2022 due to:

* global commodity price shocks,
* supply chain disruptions,
* rising fuel prices,
* and post-pandemic recovery pressures.

---

## 🦠 COVID Significantly Changed Inflation Patterns

* Food inflation surged during lockdowns due to panic buying and supply disruptions.
* Health inflation remained structurally high because of increased medical demand.
* Housing inflation initially weakened during lockdowns and recovered later.

---

## ⛽ Fuel Prices Strongly Impacted Transport Inflation

Transport showed the strongest correlation with fuel prices (~67%), proving that imported oil prices directly influence transportation costs and overall inflation.

---

## 📊 Food Inflation Was Highly Volatile

Perishable categories like:

* Vegetables
* Fruits

showed sharp month-to-month fluctuations, while:

* Milk
* Pulses

remained relatively stable.

---

# 📌 Conclusion

This project demonstrates how Microsoft Excel can be used for advanced inflation and economic analysis through data cleaning, feature engineering, trend analysis, and visualization techniques.

The analysis revealed that food is the strongest contributor to CPI inflation in India, with vegetables and meat showing the highest price pressure among food categories. It also highlighted that fuel price fluctuations have the strongest impact on transport inflation, showing the role of global oil prices in driving cost-push inflation.

Additionally, the project captured how COVID-19 disrupted inflation trends across food, healthcare, and household services, followed by gradual stabilization during the post-pandemic recovery period.

Overall, this project strengthened my understanding of CPI-based inflation analysis, economic indicators, and real-world data storytelling using Excel.
